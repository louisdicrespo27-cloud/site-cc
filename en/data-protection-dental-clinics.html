Data protection (GDPR) for dental clinics

Dental clinics operating in Portugal process health data — a special category of data subject to enhanced protection. This practice area describes legal consultancy and audit on data protection under Portuguese law, with a written legal opinion. It does not include acting as Data Protection Officer (DPO).

The legal framework

Day to day, a dental clinic processes patient identification, clinical records, radiographs, treatment plans, billing and communications. Where those data reveal information about health, they fall within the special category under Article 9 of the GDPR, with stricter rules on lawfulness, security and accountability.

In Portugal, the framework combines the General Data Protection Regulation with Lei n.º 58/2019 (the Portuguese data protection act) and other applicable legislation. The principle of accountability requires the controller to be able to demonstrate compliance — it is not enough to say that the clinic “has a privacy policy”.

For clinics and oral healthcare providers, the practical questions are usually: what processing takes place, on what legal basis, which suppliers are involved, what risks arise, and what documentation supports the decisions taken.

The service in stages

Legal support is usually organised in stages. Each clinic starts from a different point; the concrete scope is fixed after an initial assessment.

1

Audit

Mapping of processing activities, data flows, legal bases, suppliers and existing security measures.

2

Report and legal opinion

A written document with findings, material legal risks and a reasoned opinion on the state of compliance.

3

Action plan

Prioritisation of corrective and preventive measures, in a practical order suited to the size of the clinic.

4

Training (optional)

A session for the team on good practice in processing patient data and handling incidents.

5

Legal follow-up (optional)

Limited support on document reviews, data-subject requests or incidents, without acting as Data Protection Officer.

Deliverables

Depending on the agreed scope, the clinic may receive working documentation and legal guidance, for example:

  • A Record of Processing Activities aligned with the clinic’s reality.
  • Privacy notices and information for patients, where needed.
  • Processor contracts or clauses with suppliers that process data.
  • An internal plan for responding to personal data breaches.
  • Training materials or a session for the team, if requested.

Deliverables describe the legal work performed; they are not a “commercial package” and do not replace the controller’s own responsibility.
